Frequently Asked Questions

When should my child visit a dentist for the first time?

The first dental visit should be by the age of 1 or within six months of the first tooth erupting to ensure proper oral health.

How often should my child see a pediatric dentist?

A dental check-up every 6 months is recommended to prevent cavities and monitor oral development.

Is fluoride treatment safe for kids?

Yes, fluoride treatments are safe and effective in preventing tooth decay when applied in controlled amounts by a dentist.

What can I do if my child has a fear of dentists?

Dr. Nirali Mehta specializes in behavioral dentistry, using gentle techniques, sedation, and a friendly environment to help children feel relaxed during treatment.

What is the best way to prevent cavities in kids?

Ensure regular brushing with fluoride toothpaste, limit sugary snacks, and visit a pediatric dentist for fluoride & sealant applications.

Does Curis Life provide emergency dental care for children?

Yes, we offer 24/7 emergency dental care for tooth injuries, knocked-out teeth, and sudden dental pain.
