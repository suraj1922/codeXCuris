Frequently Asked Questions

What are common symptoms requiring gynecologist visit?

Irregular periods, severe cramps, unusual discharge, pelvic pain, abnormal bleeding, infertility concerns, or menopause-related issues.

How can I improve my chances of getting pregnant naturally?

Maintain a healthy lifestyle, eat a balanced diet, exercise regularly, track ovulation, and manage stress. Consult a gynecologist if you have been trying for over a year.

What should I expect during my first gynecologist visit?

Your first visit may include a detailed medical history review, a physical examination, discussion about menstrual cycles, birth control options, and any concerns you may have regarding reproductive health. A Pap smear or pelvic ultrasound may be recommended if necessary.

What lifestyle changes can help manage PCOS (Polycystic Ovary Syndrome)?

PCOS can be managed through weight management, a healthy diet (low in processed sugars), regular exercise, and hormonal therapy if needed. Your gynecologist may also recommend medications to regulate periods and control symptoms.

How do I know if I’m going through menopause?

Menopause typically occurs between 45-55 years and is marked by symptoms like hot flashes, night sweats, mood swings, vaginal dryness, and irregular periods. A gynecologist can help manage symptoms with hormone therapy, lifestyle adjustments, and nutritional guidance.
